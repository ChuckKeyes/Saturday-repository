# B52 & F14 & F4
The Rockwell B-1 Lancer is a supersonic variable-sweep wing, heavy bomber used by the United States Air Force. It is one of the Air Force's three strategic bombers, along with the B-2 Spirit and the B-52 Stratofortress. The B-1 is highly versatile, carrying the largest payload of both guided and unguided weapons in the Air Force inventory. It is capable of reaching a top speed of Mach 1.25 and can fly intercontinental ranges without stopping to refuel. Nicknamed "The Bone," the B-1 has flown more than 12,000 sorties since 2001.
